Quokkaville Buzz: A Spooky Success!

By Finnegan Longtail, Reporter

Quokkaville came alive last night with giggles, squeals, and the rustle of candy bags as children of all ages went door-to-door for Halloween! Streets were lined with spooky decorations, jack-o’-lanterns flickering, and the air filled with the sweet scent of treats.

Adding an extra dose of charm to the festivities, Mayor McQuokka joined in the fun, accompanied by his daughters Sasha and Rosie. The trio made quite the impression, with the mayor dressed as a friendly wizard and the girls twirling in glittery costumes, delighting everyone they met.

From haunted houses to glowing pumpkins, every corner of town was a scene straight from a storybook. Local families say it was the best Halloween in years, and the mayor promised next year would be even bigger. One thing’s for sure—Quokkaville knows how to trick, treat, and sparkle!

October 31th, 2025

Gannegan “Gangly” Longtail Joins The Daily Smile

By Nina Newshound, Editor-in-Chief

The Daily Smile is beaming a little brighter this week with the arrival of its newest reporter, Gannegan “Gangly” Longtail. Known around Quokkaville for his quick wit and boundless curiosity, Gangly brings an energetic spark to the newsroom. With his trademark crooked grin and knack for finding stories in the most unexpected corners, he’s already turning heads — and filling notebooks.

Editor-in-Chief Nina Newshound praised Gangly’s “keen nose for a headline and heart for the truth.” Gangly himself says he’s thrilled to join the Smile, especially alongside his cousin Finnegan Longtail. “Between the two of us,” he joked, “there won’t be a single untold story left in Quokkaville — or an unguarded cookie jar.”

Readers can look forward to Gangly’s first feature in next week’s edition. If his enthusiasm is any clue, it’s bound to make us all smile a little wider.
