Mystery of the Missing Cookies:
Part One

Back to Nettle Brightpaw Books

Juniper, a small, sprightly quokka with a tuft of golden fur on his head, bounced eagerly around the cozy little village of Quokkaville. The sun shone warmly over the cobblestone streets, and the scent of freshly baked treats drifted from every window. But today, something was terribly, terribly wrong.

“Rosie!” Juniper called, rushing to the bakery at the corner of Maple Street. “Something awful has happened!”

Rosie, a cheerful quokka with rosy cheeks and a bright red scarf, looked up from arranging the display of cupcakes. “What is it, Juniper?”

“They… they’re gone!” Juniper cried, his little paws trembling. “The cookies! All the cookies from Mr. Crumble’s bakery—they’ve disappeared!”

Rosie gasped. “Disappeared? But… who could do such a thing?”

Juniper’s best friends, Sasha and Tilly, arrived just as Juniper was pacing back and forth. Sasha, ever the practical quokka, crossed her paws and raised an eyebrow. “Calm down, Juniper. Let’s think. When did you last see them?”

Juniper’s ears drooped. “This morning! I was helping Mr. Crumble mix the dough, and by the time I came back from delivering the tray to the counter… poof! Gone! Not even a crumb!”

Tilly’s eyes sparkled with curiosity. “A mystery!” she squeaked. “We must find the cookie culprit!”

And so, the four friends—Juniper, Rosie, Sasha, and Tilly—set off to investigate. They started at the bakery, sniffing the air for clues. Juniper twitched his nose. “Hmm… I smell… chocolate?”

Sasha sniffed carefully. “And vanilla. But also… something else. Something… sneaky.”

Tilly bounced excitedly. “Let’s follow the trail!”

The scent of cookies led them out of the bakery and down the winding streets of Quokkaville. They passed Mr. Crumble, who was frantically waving a flour-dusted hand. “Have you seen my cookies? My prize-winning double-chocolate chip cookies? Gone! All gone!”

“We’ll find them, Mr. Crumble!” Juniper promised.

The friends followed the faint, sweet smell of chocolate and sugar toward the edge of the village, where the forest of Rainbowroot Trees began. The trees shimmered with colors of every hue, and tiny sparkles of magic danced in the air.

“Are you sure this is the right way?” Rosie asked, looking at the twisting, glittering path.

Juniper nodded. “The scent is strongest this way!”

Soon, they stumbled upon a curious sight: tiny footprints leading toward a hollow log. The footprints were small, round, and suspiciously paw-shaped. Juniper’s eyes widened. “Cookies thieves have paws! Or… maybe a very messy squirrel.”

Tilly peered into the hollow log. “Hello? Anyone in there?” she called. But only the sound of rustling leaves answered back.

Sasha examined the footprints. “They seem to go in… but not out. That’s strange.”

Juniper, determined not to be outsmarted, crawled a little way into the hollow. Suddenly, a cloud of flour puffed into the air, making everyone sneeze. “Aha!” Juniper exclaimed. “There’s a trail of cookie dust!”

Following the cookie dust deeper into the hollow, they discovered a tiny door carved into the side of the log. It was just big enough for a small creature to enter. And hanging from the doorframe was a sign, written in what looked like chocolate syrup: “NO QUOKKAS ALLOWED.”

Rosie tilted her head. “That’s rude. But… it looks like our cookie culprit lives inside.”

Juniper took a deep breath. “Let’s investigate carefully. We don’t know what we’re dealing with.”

The friends tiptoed into the log and found themselves in a cozy little room, filled with crumbs, cookie jars, and… a tiny table set for tea. And in the center, a small figure wearing a cloak and a hat sat munching a cookie.

“Excuse me!” Juniper called, trying to sound brave. “Are you… are you the one who took all the cookies?”

The figure froze, then slowly turned around. It was… a tiny raccoon, with bright eyes and a mischievous grin.

“I… I just love cookies,” the raccoon said softly. “I didn’t mean to take them all. I just… couldn’t resist.”

Rosie’s frown softened. “But all of Mr. Crumble’s cookies? That’s a lot!”

The raccoon looked down, ashamed. “I know… I only meant to take one or two. I didn’t think I’d eat them all!”

Sasha crossed her paws. “Well… we need to return them, or Mr. Crumble will be very upset.”

The raccoon nodded eagerly. “I’ll help!”

Together, they began to carry the cookie jars back toward the village. But just as they reached the entrance of Rainbowroot Forest, a shadow fell across the path.

Juniper looked up and froze. High in the branches of a glittering tree, two glowing eyes watched them. A low growl rumbled from somewhere above.

“What… what is that?” Tilly whispered, clutching Juniper’s arm.

The raccoon froze, its ears twitching. “I… I don’t know. I’ve never seen it before.”

The growl grew louder, echoing through the forest. Leaves rustled as the shadow moved closer. Juniper swallowed hard. “Friends… maybe we should… um… run?”

But before they could decide, the glowing eyes leapt down toward them, and a gust of sparkling wind swept through the trees. The cookie jars wobbled dangerously in their paws.

Rosie held tight to a jar. “Juniper! Do something!”

Juniper’s mind raced. He had no plan. No strategy. Just courage—and maybe a little luck. “We… we have to hide!” he shouted.

The four friends dove behind a cluster of rainbow-colored mushrooms, the raccoon scrambling after them. The glowing eyes circled, sniffing the air, and the low growl continued.

Juniper peeked out from behind the mushrooms. The shadowed figure moved closer and… then suddenly, it disappeared into the mist. The friends held their breath.

“Did it… go away?” Sasha whispered.

Juniper shook his head. “I don’t know. But we can’t just leave the cookies here. We need to get them back to Mr. Crumble.”

The raccoon tugged on Juniper’s sleeve. “Maybe… maybe it’s guarding something. Or someone. I… I think it wanted the cookies too.”

Juniper’s eyes widened. “Or… maybe it’s trying to stop us from finding out who really took them!”

Tilly shivered. “You mean… there’s an even bigger mystery?”

Juniper nodded. “I think so. And I have a feeling… we’re only at the beginning.”

The friends gathered the cookie jars as quietly as they could, glancing nervously at the shadows between the trees. The forest seemed to whisper around them, carrying secrets they couldn’t yet understand.

As they stepped cautiously out of Rainbowroot Forest, the glowing eyes reappeared on a distant hilltop, staring right at them. And then, in a voice that sounded like a mix of wind and leaves, it whispered:

“You’re getting warmer… but you’ll never find all the cookies… unless you dare follow me.”

Juniper swallowed. His heart raced. He looked at his friends, who all nodded bravely, even though their paws were trembling.

“Friends,” he said, gripping a cookie jar, “I think… the mystery isn’t over. Not by a long shot.”

And with that, the shadow melted into the mist, leaving the friends standing at the edge of the forest, the cookie jars in their paws, and the biggest question of all: who—or what—was really behind the missing cookies?

The sun dipped lower in the sky, casting long shadows over Quokkaville. And as the wind rustled through the Rainbowroot Trees, Juniper and his friends knew one thing for certain: the adventure had only just begun.

And somewhere deep in the forest, glowing eyes watched. Waiting.
